Why Automated Meeting Summarization?

  • 📋
    Knowledge Loss — Decisions made in meetings are poorly captured; manual notes are incomplete and inconsistent.
  • 🔒
    Privacy Constraints — Corporate, healthcare, and legal meetings cannot be routed through cloud APIs.
  • ⚡
    Scale Problem — Professionals spend 30–50% of workdays in meetings; summary generation must be automatic.
  • 🔍
    Actionability Gap — Recordings are not searchable or actionable; structured MoM outputs enable follow-up.

Our Solution

A fully offline, end-to-end pipeline that converts raw AMI meeting audio into:

🎙️ Speaker-attributed transcripts
📄 Minutes of Meeting (MoM) narratives
✅ Structured decisions & action items
📊 Reproducible evaluation metrics

Running on a GTX 1080 Ti workstation — no cloud required.

Speech Processing Stack — NVIDIA NeMo

🔊 VAD

Model: MarbleNet (.nemo)
Output: vad_segments.json, .rttm

Labels each frame as speech or nonspeech. Filters non-speech before diarization and ASR.

👥 Diarization

Model: MSDD (inference.yaml)
Output: diarization_segments.json, .rttm

Speaker-attributed time segments. Embeddings cached for repeated runs. Parameter-tuned via DER.

🎯 ASR

Models: Conformer CTC S/M/L
Output: asr_segments.json, full_transcript.txt

Speaker-aware transcription. Real confidence scores extracted from log-probs.

Evaluation Methodology

🎯 WER — Word Error Rate
Standard ASR metric: (Substitutions + Deletions + Insertions) / Reference Words.
Computed per meeting and aggregated. Written to wer_scores.csv.
👥 cpWER — Permutation WER
Speaker-aware WER. Concatenates per-speaker utterances, finds minimum-WER speaker assignment across all permutations. More realistic end-to-end metric.
📊 DER — Diarization Error Rate
Approximate no-overlap interval method. Suitable for comparative tuning. Documented Caveat: not full pyannote.metrics
📝 ROUGE
ROUGE-1, ROUGE-2, ROUGE-L against AMI reference summaries when available. Reference-optional; fields remain empty otherwise.
🔊 ASR Confidence QA
Real per-segment confidence scores (from NeMo log-probs). Mean/min statistics, low-confidence flagging, per-meeting confidence reports.
✅ MoM Quality Checks
Schema validation, decision/action counts, uncertainty flags. Written to extraction_validation_report.json with schema_valid: true/false.

Defense Q&A: Prepared Responses

Q: Largest failure mode?
Diarization error cascading into MoM speaker attribution. When diarization misattributes a turn (~12–16% DER), action item owners are wrong even when action text is correct. Evidence chunk IDs remain accurate — the text is right, the social attribution is wrong.
Q: Where does diarization error hurt most?
High-overlap discussion segments and decision confirmation exchanges. "A proposes → B agrees" patterns with speaker confusion lose the agreement attribution. 3/11 ES2005a decisions had partially mis-attributed speaker context.
Q: How stable are llama.cpp outputs?
Functionally stable. 3 repeated ES2005a runs: 100% identical decision + action sets in 5/6 meetings. 1 borderline decision (conf ~0.52) alternated — expected threshold behavior. Temperature=0.05 minimizes variability.
Q: What if GPU nondeterminism were eliminated?
artifact_digest becomes strictly byte-identical. Functional outputs unchanged — GPU nondeterminism affects low-order float bits, not semantic decisions at temperature=0.05. Main beneficiary: governance audit layer.
Q: Can this generalize beyond AMI?
Stages 3–10 are directly reusable. NeMo speech models and llama.cpp NLP generalize to any English meeting audio. AMI-specific: annotation ingestion, eval format adapters. Non-AMI deployment needs format adaptation for evaluation only.
Q: If deployed in enterprise, what breaks first?
1. Audio format diversity (MP4/M4A/Teams) — needs FFmpeg preprocessing.
2. Speaker scale — MSDD degrades above 8 speakers.
3. Domain vocabulary — high OOV WER for technical/legal/medical terms.

Limitations & Future Work

Current Limitations

  • ⚠️
    GPU Nondeterminism — Byte-identical reproducibility not guaranteed for CUDA NeMo / llama.cpp paths. Risk tracked and auditable.
  • ⚠️
    Single-Channel Audio — Uses Mix-Headset mono mix. Individual headset channels could improve per-speaker ASR.
  • ⚠️
    Approximate DER — No-overlap interval method. Not equivalent to pyannote.metrics canonical DER.
  • ⚠️
    ROUGE Reference Dependency — Requires AMI MoM reference documents; often unavailable.
  • ⚠️
    English-Only — All models tuned for English. No multilingual support.
